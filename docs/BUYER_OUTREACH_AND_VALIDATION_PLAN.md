# Buyer Outreach and Validation Plan

## Goal
Move BlackCrest from a codebase-only asset into a strategic acquisition candidate by collecting market validation, buyer feedback, partner interest, and pilot conversations.

## Target Outcome
Within 30 days, collect:
- 25 strategic buyer conversations started
- 10 procurement/ERP feedback responses
- 5 written validation quotes
- 3 serious pilot or partner conversations
- 1 letter of intent or implementation partner discussion

## Target Buyer Segments

### ERP Implementation Firms
Pitch: Add AI procurement intelligence to your existing ERP implementation and advisory practice.

### Procurement Consulting Firms
Pitch: Turn procurement diagnostics and sourcing improvement work into recurring software-driven revenue.

### GovCon Advisory Firms
Pitch: Give defense suppliers better procurement risk, sourcing, and operational readiness visibility.

### Supply-Chain SaaS Vendors
Pitch: Expand your existing product into decision intelligence, margin leakage, and supplier risk analytics.

### Manufacturing Technology Integrators
Pitch: Offer manufacturers an intelligence layer above purchasing, supplier, and operational data.

## Short Outreach Message
Subject: ERP-connected procurement intelligence platform

I’m validating BlackCrest Procurement Intelligence OS, an early-stage platform foundation that turns ERP-style procurement data into supplier risk visibility, margin leakage alerts, late delivery exposure, sourcing actions, and executive reporting.

I’m not reaching out for a generic software pitch. I’m looking for feedback from people who understand procurement, ERP, sourcing, or manufacturing operations.

The strategic angle is simple: a buyer or partner with existing ERP/procurement distribution could commercialize this faster than building from zero.

Would you be open to a 10-minute review of the walkthrough and giving blunt feedback?

## Strategic Buyer Message
Subject: Strategic acquisition / licensing opportunity in procurement intelligence

BlackCrest Procurement Intelligence OS is an early-stage ERP-connected procurement intelligence platform foundation built for manufacturers, GovCon suppliers, distributors, and sourcing teams.

It is designed to identify supplier risk, margin leakage, late delivery exposure, sourcing opportunities, and executive-level procurement insights from ERP-style procurement data.

I’m exploring whether the right strategic buyer, implementation partner, or software operator would rather acquire/license the foundation than build from scratch.

There are two separate structures: codebase-only acquisition and broader strategic platform rights. The larger valuation applies only to commercialization rights, product foundation, documentation, roadmap, demo assets, and structured handoff.

## Validation Questions
1. Is supplier risk and margin leakage visibility a real pain point for your clients or team?
2. Would this be more valuable as a standalone SaaS, white-label tool, or consulting/implementation add-on?
3. What ERP systems should this integrate with first?
4. What would make this credible enough for a pilot?
5. What would a buyer need to see before considering acquisition or licensing?
6. Who inside an organization would own this purchase?
7. What pricing model would make sense?

## Proof To Collect
- Written quotes from procurement leaders
- Screenshots of replies
- Meeting notes from calls
- Buyer objections
- Names of ERP systems mentioned
- Common use cases
- Pilot interest
- Partner interest

## Do Not Say
- This is a finished enterprise SaaS company.
- This already has live customer ERP integrations.
- This already has revenue if it does not.
- The code alone is worth $1.2M.

## Say Instead
BlackCrest is an early-stage procurement intelligence product foundation. The codebase-only sale is one option. The larger strategic valuation applies to broader commercialization rights, documentation, roadmap, product positioning, and the opportunity for a buyer with distribution to commercialize it.
